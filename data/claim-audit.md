Evidence type legend:

- `synthetic`: token-target benchmark rows from harness CSV/normalized tables.
- `agent workload`: OpenCode scenario runs; single-run, path-dependent unless noted.
- `Prometheus/DCGM`: exported metric JSON or local Prometheus computations.
- `run outcome/logs`: completion status, pass/fail, runtime notes, or source report-draft summaries.
- `extrapolation`: inference from measured data, not directly tested.
- `operator/source note`: prior draft statement or manual context, not independently validated here.

Strength legend:

- `strong`: directly supported by named run data and appropriate evidence type.
- `moderate`: supported directionally but needs scope/caveat due to sample size, mixed evidence, or extrapolation.
- `weak`: plausible but too interpretive or under-instrumented.
- `unsupported`: no evidence found in audited local sources, or evidence contradicts the wording.

## Evidence Boundary Notes

- Synthetic evidence supports runtime behavior for controlled prompt sizes, success rates, wall latency, and measured VRAM/power where available. It does not support answer quality.
- Agent workload evidence supports operational completion and end-to-end wall time for specific OpenCode scenarios. It does not support pure GPU throughput, reproducible speedup ratios, or model quality.
- Prometheus-confirmed facts are strongest for RTX 5090 and Ada Q6. Ada Q4 phases 1-3 use pre-computed summaries with full raw TSDB in the private archive, and dual RTX 5090 lacks local DCGM, TTFT, and valid llama.cpp metrics.
- Routing evidence for dual RTX 5090 is client-timing evidence plus a successful benchmark run. It is not enough to prove stable multi-GPU capacity or balanced routing.
- Runtime viability means requests/scenarios completed without reported crash/OOM in the tested envelope. It does not mean answer correctness, coding quality, reasoning quality, or production SLO readiness.
